# FUTURE_DS_02 – Customer Retention & Churn Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn and retention patterns using a telecommunications subscription dataset. The objective was to identify why customers leave, which segments are most at risk, and what business actions can improve retention.

This type of analysis is widely used in SaaS and subscription-based companies where reducing churn directly impacts revenue growth.

---

## 🛠 Tools Used
- Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Data Cleaning & Transformation (Power Query)

---

## 📊 Key Metrics Created
- Total Customers
- Churned Customers
- Churn Rate %
- Average Tenure
- Average Monthly Charges

---

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### Executive Overview:
- Overall churn rate analysis
- Churn rate by contract type
- Churn rate by tenure
- Churn rate by internet service
- Customer distribution by churn status

### Retention Deep Dive:
- Churn by contract type
- Churn by payment method
- Tenure distribution by churn
- Monthly charges comparison (Churn vs Non-Churn)
- Segment-level filtering using slicers

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Month-to-month contracts show significantly higher churn rates compared to one-year and two-year contracts.
- Customers with lower tenure (early lifecycle stage) are more likely to churn.
- Fiber optic internet users show higher churn rates compared to DSL and non-internet users.
- Customers with higher monthly charges demonstrate increased churn likelihood.
- Long-term contract customers (two-year plans) have the lowest churn rate.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Incentivize customers to shift from month-to-month to longer-term contracts.
- Strengthen onboarding programs during the first 6–12 months.
- Review pricing strategy for high monthly charge segments.
- Improve service quality or support for fiber optic customers.
- Develop targeted retention campaigns for high-risk segments.
